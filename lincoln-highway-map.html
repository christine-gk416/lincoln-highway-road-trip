Your Journey Down Route 30

Pittsburgh

Start your trip in Pittsburgh, the second largest city in Pennsylvania. This city is located in the far southwestern part of the state, on the Allegheny plateau. Many of its distinctive neighborhoods are built right into the these mountainous foothills. Other parts of Pittsburgh are shaped around its iconic three rivers: the Ohio, Monongahela, and Allegheny. Pittsburgh was once one of the most industrialised cities in the world, but in the post-industrial era it has focused on becoming a hub of higher education.


With the Andy Warhol and Carnegie museums located in the city, Pittsburgh is also considered an artistic stronghold in Pennsylvania. It’s regularly listed as the most livable city in the US.

Things to do in Pittsburgh

Back to map

Where to eat:

  • Try Pittsburgh’s signature sandwich, with fries on it at Primanti Bros.
  • Gaucho Parrilla Argentina regularly ranks as a local favourite steakhouse.
  • For more local flavour and brews, check out The Church Brew Works.

More things to do:

  • Encounter plants and flowers from around the world at the Phipps Conservatory.
  • Immerse yourself in the installation art pieces at the The Matress Factory.
  • Walk with the dinosaurs at Carnegie Museum of Natural History.

Where to eat:

  • You can grab a picnic lunch after a tour of the house at the Fallingwater Café.
  • For a sit-down meal and a pint, head to the nearby Falls City Pub.
  • Enjoy healthy farm to table fare at the Bittersweet Café.

More things to do:

  • Of course, take a tour of the Fallingwater House and go hiking at Bear Run Nature Reserve.
  • If you're feeling adventurous, go white water rafting in Ohiopyle with White Water Adventures.
  • Visit the Flight 93 Memorial before looping back up onto the main route 30.

Things to do around Bedford

Back to map

Where to eat:

  • Stop at the Jean Bonnet Tavern, where George Washington may have stayed, for American pub fair.
  • Try Horn O Plenty for a healthy and fresh-grown brunch.
  • For a special occasion, check out the carefully chosen menu at 10/09.

More things to do:

  • There are multiple options for free walking tours of the modern town of Bedford, either guided or self-paced.
  • Explore the history of western PA by touring old Bedford Village, a reconstruction of the 18th-19th century town.
  • Take a step back from the hustle and bustle of the road and enjoy a spa day at Springs Eternal Spa.

Where to eat:


  • Located in a ivy-covered brick building, Warehouse Gourmet: Bistro & Brew Pub is a romantic place to stop for dinner.
  • Taste classic central Pennsylvania cuisine in an 160 year-old restored barn at Hickory Bridge Farm Restaurant.
  • Looking for a place right on the town square to catch a game? Try Blue & Gray Bar: Bar & Grill.

More things to do:

  • Mr. Ed's Emporium is an extensive candy shop and musuem of elephants figures collected by the owner over a lifetime.
  • Of course, take a ghost tour after dark through Gettysburg Ghost Tours, or try the Craft Beer and Spirits Tour.

Things to do in Harrisburg

Back to map

Where to eat:

  • See all over downtown Harrisburg city from the roof of the original Appalachian Brewing Company.
  • Stop for a quick, hearty, and delicious lunch at local favourite, Neato Burrito.
  • Browse local art, watch the artists at work, and enjoy carefully crafted local-grown food at Millworks.

More things to do:

  • Enjoy an afternoon of outdoor activities at City Island.
  • Take a tour of the Fort Hunter Mansion and have a picnic in the park, further north along the Susquehanna River.
  • Learn about the history and famous art of Pennsylvania at the State Museum of Pennsylvania.

Where to eat:

  • Stop right in downtown Lancaster for a lovely meal and cocktail at The Pressroom.
  • Try the Prince Street Café for a light lunch and the best cup of coffee in town.
  • Take a detour over to route 322 to eat at the Amish and Pennsylvania Dutch Smorgasbord at Shady Maple.

More things to do:

  • Peruse local produce and homemade products at Lancaster Central Market, the oldest continuously open farmers market in the US.
  • Catch a play or hear the Lancaster Symphony Orchestra at the Fulton Theatre.
  • Immerse yourself in the Amish way of life at the Amish Farm & House.

Things to do in Philadelphia

Back to map

Where to eat:

  • We're not qualified to make a call in the great Philly cheeseteak debate, so we'll let you pick between Pat's and Geno's.
  • The Plough and the Stars is Philly's premiere Irish pub (with a Philly flair), owned by Irish emmigrants.
  • Head into Chinatown for a duck feast at Sang Kee Peking Duck House.

More things to do:

  • If you have an interest in medical history, or a general interest in the macabre, you've gotta see the Mütter Musuem.
  • Walk the same halls where Al Capone was once locked up at Eastern State Penitentiary.
  • Relax by the Delaware River in the Spruce Street Harbor Park.
